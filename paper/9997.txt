Individual Preferences, Monetary Gambles and the Equity Premium
Nicholas Barberis, Ming Huang, and Richard Thaler
NBER Working Paper No. 9997
September 2003
JEL No. D1, D8, G11, G12
ABSTRACT
We argue that narrow framing, whereby an agent who is offered a new gamble evaluates that gamble
in isolation, separately from other risks she already faces, may be a more important feature of
decision-making under risk than previously realized. To demonstrate this, we present evidence on
typical attitudes to independent monetary gambles with both large and small stakes and show that
across a wide range of utility functions, including all expected utility and many non-expected utility
specifications, the only ones that can easily capture these attitudes are precisely those exhibiting
narrow framing. Our analysis also makes predictions about the kinds of preferences that might be
able to address the stock market participation and equity premium puzzles. We illustrate these
predictions in simple portfolio choice and equilibrium settings.
